# Unilever Nepal Fleet Tracking - Sales Manager Demo Script

## 1. Introduction (The Scoped View)
"Hello team. We just looked at the Admin view, which sees everything. But a Sales Manager in Pokhara doesn't need to see trucks heading to Biratnagar. They need focus. 

This is the Sales Manager Dashboard. It is 'scoped' specifically to the manager's assigned clients and regions. We've removed the noise like engine idling metrics or chassis numbers. Here, it's all about Customer Experience and Delivery ETAs."

## 2. Order Status Board (Kanban View)
"Right away, you'll notice the layout is different. We are using a Kanban-style layout organized by delivery status: Dispatched, In Transit, and Arriving. 

Sales managers can instantly see which of their customers' orders are moving. If a top-tier distributor is waiting for a restock, the manager can see exactly where that truck is without having to call the logistics team."

## 3. Proactive Delay Management
"Things go wrong—traffic jams happen. But the worst thing we can do is let the customer find out they are delayed by waiting endlessly. 

Notice the delay indicators on the cards. When a truck falls behind schedule, it's flagged here immediately. This empowers the Sales Manager to proactively call the distributor, apologize for the delay, and provide a revised ETA *before* the customer even has to ask. This flips a negative experience into a display of excellent customer service."

## 4. One-Click Customer Communication
"Speaking of customer service, let's look at the 'Send Tracking' feature. 

Instead of taking phone calls asking 'Where is my delivery?', the Sales Manager can click a single button to generate a secure SMS tracking link. This link is sent directly to the distributor's phone. 

It takes all the manual work out of updates and puts the power directly in the customer's hands."

## Conclusion
"The Sales Dashboard bridges the gap between logistics and customer relations. By providing sales managers with targeted, real-time delivery data, they can manage client expectations perfectly and reduce friction in the supply chain."
